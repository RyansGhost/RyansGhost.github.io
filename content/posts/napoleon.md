Napoleon Bonaparte, born in 1769, rose to prominence during France's turbulent Revolutionary period. His military genius reshaped European politics through campaigns across the continent. While often depicted as a tyrant, Napoleon implemented significant reforms including the Napoleonic Code which established modern civil law systems. His 1812 invasion of Russia marked a turning point - though ultimately successful in capturing Moscow, he suffered catastrophic losses from the Russian winter. This article explores how Napoleon's military strategies revolutionized warfare and how his complex legacy continues to influence politics today.
